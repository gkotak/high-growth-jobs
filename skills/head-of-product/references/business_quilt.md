# Jurgen Appelo’s Business Quilt (Reference)

This reference outlines the **Business Quilt** framework for managing startup growth. Use this to categorize the user's current status and propose the next necessary "patch" to validate.

## The 4 Lifecycle Stages (Horizontal)
1.  **Discovery (Ideation):**
    *   *Goal:* Problem/Solution Fit.
    *   *Focus:* Desirability (Do they want it?).
    *   *Exit Criteria:* Validated customer problem and a theoretical solution that solves it.
2.  **Validation (Experimentation):**
    *   *Goal:* Product/Market Fit (PMF).
    *   *Focus:* Viability (Will they pay?) & Feasibility (Can we build MVP?).
    *   *Exit Criteria:* Repeatable sales/usage, working MVP.
3.  **Efficiency (Scaling):**
    *   *Goal:* Unit Economics & Growth.
    *   *Focus:* Optimization.
    *   *Exit Criteria:* Positive LTV:CAC, automated operations.
4.  **Sustainability (Stability):**
    *   *Goal:* Longevity & Moats.
    *   *Focus:* Defense.
    *   *Exit Criteria:* Defensible market position, renewal/expansion.

## The 3 Perspectives (Vertical)
1.  **The Model (Desirability):** Customer Segments, Value Prop, Channels.
2.  **The Engine (Feasibility):** Key Activities, Partners, Resources, Team.
3.  **The Math (Viability):** Cost Structure, Revenue Streams, Pricing.

## How to Apply
*   **Diagnose:** Ask "Which stage are we in?" (Usually Discovery or Validation for early startups).
*   **Identify Gaps:** Look for "Empty Patches". E.g., "We have a Solution (Engine) but no Value Prop (Model)."
*   **Propose Experiments:** To fill a patch, run an experiment. "We assume CFOs will pay $500/mo. Test: Try to sell 5 pre-orders."
